The Session Structure (every lesson)

Follow this format for all 21 lessons
▾
Listen First
3–4 min
Ask your partner to say today's phrases naturally while doing the action or pointing to the thing. You listen. Don't repeat yet. Your job is to connect sound to meaning.
Absorb the Chunks
6–8 min
Read the lesson's phrase table. Say each phrase out loud 3 times, slowly. Don't think about grammar. These are chunks — whole units you absorb like a child learns "I don't know" before knowing what "don't" means.
Use it for Real
5–8 min
Do the activity with your child. Use only the target phrases. Gesture freely. If you forget a word, gesture + say the Hungarian word you do know. Your partner praises meaning, not accuracy.
Golden rule: Your partner should never correct your grammar mid-sentence. They can model the right form by repeating it naturally ("Igen, a parkba megyünk!") — this is how children absorb corrections without shame.

Practice Activity real life
The Announcement Game
Before you leave the house for anywhere — shop, park, school — announce it in Hungarian. Point at the door. "A boltba megyünk." Your child repeats it. When you reach the crossing say "Megállj! Nézz körbe!" and wait for them to look before saying "Átmehetünk."
Pattern notice: "-ba / -be" at the end of a place means "into/to" that place. You don't need to know the grammar rule — just notice that park → parkba, bolt → boltba. Your brain will absorb the pattern through repetition.

Key Insight child development
Why this matters linguistically
Consequence language is emotionally loaded — which means it gets remembered fast. "Elég!" said firmly once in context will stick in your memory better than 20 vocabulary drills. Use it authentically in real moments. Your child will also pick up on your consistency.

Build a consistent mealtime ritual entirely in Hungarian: call everyone with "Gyertek enni!", say "Jó étvágyat!" before eating, ask "Kérsz még?" mid-meal, and end with "Köszönöm az ételt." Szórakoztató volt.Só-ro-koz-to-tó voltIt was fun.

Practice Activity end of day
The Feelings Check-In
At dinner or before bed, everyone at the table answers "Milyen volt a napod?" in Hungarian — you, your partner, and your child. Even one word counts: "Jó." "Fáradt." This makes it a family ritual rather than a lesson, which is exactly where language lives best.
End every day with "Szeretlek." It costs nothing to say, requires no grammar knowledge, and will be the most remembered phrase in this entire course.
A note on pronunciation: Hungarian is phonetically very consistent — once you know how each letter sounds, you can pronounce any word. The sounds to focus on first: gy (like "dy"), sz (like "s"), cs (like "ch"), and the long vowels á, é, í, ó, ő, ú, ű which are held longer than their short versions. Ask your partner to say each phrase first — your best pronunciation resource is already in the room.
